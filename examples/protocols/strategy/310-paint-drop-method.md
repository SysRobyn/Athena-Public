# Protocol 310: The Paint Drop Method (Skill Stacking & Career Strategy)

> **Source**: Taylor Pearson, "The Paint Drop Method"
> **Purpose**: Developing a unique, uncommon skill set in a high-leverage economy.
> **Type**: Career Strategy / Strategic Planning

---

## 1. The Paint Drop Mental Model

Imagine a paintbrush swiped across a wall.

- **The Swipe**: Broad, interdisciplinary understanding. Knowledge of many things at a basic level (L1/L2).
- **The Drops**: Specializations. Most drops are short (mild specialization).
- **The Long Drops**: Deep expertise (L4/L5) in a unique and valuable skill.

**Success Formula**: Interdisciplinary Breadth + 2-4 "Long Drops" (Bionic Multiplier).

---

## 2. Product-Market-Founder Fit (The Three-Box Test)

To find your "Long Drops", you must triangulate between three dimensions:

1. **Interesting (Founder Fit)**: What would you work on if you won the lottery tomorrow? What gives you "Flow"?
2. **Mastery (Product Fit)**: What do you do well? What is easy for you but hard for others?
3. **Market Value (Market Fit)**: What can you be paid for? Where is the demand?

---

## 3. The Power of Skill Stacking (The Top 25% Rule)

There are two paths to being "The Best in the World":

1. **Path A (The Tiger Woods Path)**: Be the absolute top 0.01% in ONE specific thing. (High risk, high competition).
2. **Path B (The Dilbert Path)**: Be in the **Top 25%** in 3-4 specific things and combine them. (High probability, creates a unique niche).

**Example (Scott Adams)**: Better at drawing than most + Funnier than most + Business background = Dilbert (Unique Moat).

---

## 4. Peanut Butter Skills (Compound Buffs)

Skills that "go well with everything." Mastering these increases the value of every other "Long Drop":

- **Communication**: Writing, speaking, clarifying difficult points.
- **Engineering**: Sequence thinking, understanding how systems/software work.
- **Sales & Marketing**: Getting the right people to notice and pay for your Mastery.
- **Finance & Markets**: Understanding equity, debt, and how value is captured.

---

## 5. Execution Rules

- **Comparative Advantage**: Always do more of the things you find easy and others find hard. If a skill remains excruciating after 3-4 deep dives, jump to a new field.
- **Real Artists Ship**: Consistently release work publicly. You cannot know which drops are "Long" until they are tested against the world's gravity.
- **90-Day Cycles**: Plan and execute in 90-day sprints to allow for "Pivot or Plow" decisions based on feedback.
